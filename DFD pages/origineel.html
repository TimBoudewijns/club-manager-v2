Holding the stick

Proper Stick Handling

Before mastering advanced techniques like dribbling, passing, or pushing, its crucial to understand how to hold the stick correctly:

  • Left Hand: Firm grip at the top of the stick, with fingers wrapped around (no penholder grip).
  • Right Hand: Positioned in the middle, held loosely.
  • Posture: Arms extended, stick in front, elbows apart.

Turning the Stick

  • Grip: Start with your hands in a normal grip, ensuring your stick is held comfortably.
  • Movement: Rotate the stick in your hands by twisting your wrists, either clockwise or counterclockwise depending on the move.
  • Purpose: This technique allows for quick transitions between forehand and backhand, improving ball control.
  • Execution: Keep the stick close to the ground during the turn to maintain contact with the ball, especially during dribbling or tackling situations.

Running with the ball

Running with the Ball (Dribbling)

  • Body Position: Bend your knees with an upright upper body and straight back.
  • Grip: Hold the stick in a normal grip.
  • Ball Position: Keep the ball in front and slightly to the right of your body.
  • Stick Angle: Hold the stick at a 45 angle above the ground, ensuring constant ball contact.
  • Vision: Use split vision, focusing on both the ball and the field.
  • Movement: Stay balanced and in control, adjusting speed and direction as necessary while maintaining possession.

Stopping the Ball from a Dribble

  • Stick Movement: Flip the stick over so the rounded side faces forward.
  • Positioning: Place the stick in front of the ball with space between the ball and your feet.
  • Contact: Use the flat side of the stick to cushion the balls momentum.
  • Follow-Up: After stopping the ball, be prepared for a quick follow-up action, either moving left or right for the next play.

Indian Dribble

  • Grip: Hold the stick in a normal grip, keeping elbows apart.
  • Body Movement: Minimize upper body rotation as you move the ball side to side.
  • Stick Position: Keep the stick over the ball, with rhythmic, controlled movements.
  • Ball Control: Move the ball from left to right in front of your body.
  • Vision: Maintain split vision, keeping an eye on both the ball and your surroundings.

Eccentric Dribble

  • Ball Position: Move the ball outside your body line, either to the left or right.
  • Grip: Maintain a normal grip with hands apart.
  • Control: Keep the ball further from your body compared to a regular dribble, increasing your reach.
  • Stick Position: Focus on keeping the stick low to the ground while controlling the ball at a distance.
  • Movement: Perform quick, a-rhythmic movements to throw off defenders and keep the ball out of their reach.

A-Rhythmic Dribble

  • Ball Movement: Move the ball unpredictably by alternating between rhythmic and irregular dribbling patterns.
  • Grip: Use a normal grip to maintain control, ensuring flexibility in movement.
  • Direction: Take the ball outside the body to the left, then switch it to the right in front of the body.
  • Execution: Mix up your speed and direction to confuse defenders.
  • Repetition: Continuously alternate between smooth and abrupt ball movements to keep the opponent off-balance.

Normal Slalom (Backhand-Forehand)

  • Grip: Hold the stick with a normal grip.
  • Ball Position: Keep the ball far from your feet for optimal control.
  • Hand Movement: Rotate the left hand inward (counterclockwise) for backhand, while the right hand remains loose around the stick.
  • Execution: Move the ball side-to-side in front of your body, alternating between backhand and forehand.
  • Awareness: Keep your vision on both the ball and your surroundings (split vision).

Normal Slalom (Forehand Only)

  • Grip: Maintain a normal grip on the stick.
  • Ball Position: Keep the ball at a distance from your feet for control.
  • Turning Right: Point your left elbow forward when turning to the right.
  • Footwork: Use small steps to walk around the ball as you move through the slalom.
  • Execution: Continuously move the ball from side to side, using only the forehand.
  • Awareness: Stay focused on both the ball and your environment using split vision.

Running with Direction and Tempo Changes

  • Grip: Hold the stick in a normal grip with hands apart.
  • Elbow Position: Keep your elbows apart for balance and control.
  • Ball Control: Maintain constant contact between the ball and the stick.
  • Direction Change: Swiftly change direction by pulling the ball left or walking around it to the right.
  • Tempo Shift: After a change in direction, accelerate quickly for a few steps to gain an advantage.
  • Focus: Stay attentive to both the ball and your surroundings.

Running with an Open Forehand

  • Ball Position: Keep the ball to the right of your body, mainly near the back foot.
  • Stick Technique: Open the flat side of the stick with each contact to maintain control.
  • Passing Option: Keep the ball in a position where you can pass to the right at any moment.
  • Execution: Run while maintaining control of the ball, using quick adjustments to maintain ball contact and create passing opportunities.

One-Handed Dribbling (Left and Right)

  • Arm Position: Extend your arm fully to increase reach and keep the ball out of a defenders tackle zone.
  • Wrist Action: Bend your wrist inward towards your body for better control.
  • Ball Control: Dribble with one hand to maintain possession and move the ball away from opponents.
  • Body Position: Use your body to shield the ball from the defender, placing it between them and the ball.
  • Transition: After dribbling, recover the ball on your forehand with two hands for better control.

Passing the ball

Sweep Pass

  • Grip: Hold the stick with a normal grip, hands apart.
  • Stick Position: Place the stick on the ground near your right foot.
  • Ball Position: Keep the ball level with your left foot.
  • Body Position: Point your left shoulder in the direction of play.
  • Movement: Slide the stick slightly backward along the ground, then sweep forward through the ball.
  • Follow Through: Point the stick towards the ball after passing.

Sweep Pass While Running (Rolling Ball)

  • Grip: Keep your hands apart.
  • Body Position: Turn your left shoulder towards the target.
  • Stick Movement: Sweep the stick from back to front along the ground.
  • Posture: Bend your knees and keep the stick almost horizontal for more control.
  • Follow Through: Direct the ball by pointing the stick after the pass.

Long Slap/Sweep Pass

  • Technique: Combine upper body rotation, arm swing, and wrist action for a powerful slap pass.
  • Ball Position: Keep the ball at or just ahead of your left foot, away from your body.
  • Grip: Hold both hands together at the top of the stick.
  • Movement: Rotate your upper body while making a wide, sweeping motion along the ground.
  • Follow Through: Continue the swing and shift your weight from your back leg to the front.

Backhand Long Slap/Sweep Pass

  • Ball Position: Keep the ball near or in front of your right foot, away from the body.
  • Grip: Hands together at the top of the stick, arms free.
  • Movement: Sweep the stick in a wide semicircle on the ground and through the ball.
  • Body Movement: Rotate the upper body, and shift weight from the back leg to the front.
  • Follow Through: Wrist movement and ball position determine the pass direction.

Push Pass

  • Grip: Normal grip, hands apart.
  • Body Position: Left shoulder faces the target, knees bent, weight on the right leg.
  • Ball Position: Place the ball in front of your body, to the right of your right foot.
  • Movement: Pull the stick back, then push the ball over the ground.
  • Follow Through: Shift weight to the left foot and direct the ball with the stick after passing.

Hit/Long Forehand Pass

  • Ball Position: Place the ball at or just ahead of your left foot, not too far from your body.
  • Grip: Hands together at the top of the stick.
  • Posture: Slightly bend your knees and upper body, with a straight lower back.
  • Swing: Bring the stick back, then stretch your arms out during the hit.
  • Follow Through: Shift your weight from the back to the front leg, rotating your body for maximum power.

High Push (Scoop)

  • Grip: Use a normal grip with elbows apart.
  • Ball Position: Keep the ball in front of your body, away from your feet.
  • Movement: Bend your knees deeply and place the stick diagonally under the ball.
  • Leverage: Push with your right hand and pull with your left to lift the ball.
  • Follow Through: Keep contact with the ball as long as possible and point upwards after the push.

Short Slap Shot

  • Ball Position: Keep the ball about 40-50 cm in front of you.
  • Grip: Hands together, slightly below the top of the stick for a shortened grip.
  • Movement: Use a quick wrist movement and a short backswing for the hit.
  • Posture: Maintain weight on the left leg, with your left shoulder above your knee.
  • Follow Through: Squeeze the stick at the moment of contact and point towards the ball after the shot.

Lifting the Ball

  • Ball Position: Place the ball slightly in front of your feet.
  • Stick Angle: Position the stick diagonally under the ball with the flat side facing up.
  • Execution: Use a quick wrist action to lift the ball by pushing the right hand forward while pulling the left hand towards the body.
  • Follow-Up: Keep contact with the ball as long as possible for controlled elevation, passing it over an opponents stick.

One-Touch Pass

  • Posture: Bend your knees, lean forward, and extend your arms without a backswing.
  • Movement: Pass the ball with a single hit by squeezing the stick at the point of contact.
  • Direction: The shoulder and flat side of the stick guide the direction of the pass.

Chop

  • Ball Position: Place the ball behind or near your right foot (for right-handed players).
  • Stick Technique: Swing the stick downward in a sharp, quick motion, contacting the ball near the back.
  • Execution: Chop the ball with a short hit, causing it to jump or lift over the defenders stick.
  • Follow-Up: Accelerate quickly after the chop to bypass the defender and continue your attack.

Fake Pass

  • Purpose: The fake pass is used to deceive the opponent and create space.
  • Setup: Approach the ball as if you are about to make a pass.
  • Stick Position: Hold the stick in your normal passing grip.
  • Execution: Make a convincing motion towards a pass without contacting the ball.
  • Body Movement: Fake the body motion in the direction of the pass, but then quickly redirect the ball in the opposite direction or continue dribbling.
  • Follow-Up: Use the created space to advance or pass.

Receiving the ball

Forehand Receiving

  • Stance: Stand with feet slightly apart, left foot slightly ahead of the right.
  • Posture: Bend your knees, keep your arms free, and position your left arm as if checking your watch.
  • Stick: Hold the stick loosely with your right hand, keeping it vertical and slightly above the ground.
  • Ball Control: Stop the ball in front of your right foot with the stick covering it.

Dynamic Forehand Receiving

  • Movement: Approach the ball with the stick close to the ground.
  • Stick Position: Keep the flat side of the stick in front of the body to cover the ball.
  • Body Position: Push your right shoulder down and guide the ball diagonally in front of your right foot.

Forehand Receiving to Backhand Dribble

  • Movement: Receive the ball diagonally in front of your right foot.
  • Transition: Use your left elbow to guide the ball to your left foot.
  • Wrist Action: Rotate your right wrist so the right elbow points outward, and transition to dribbling on the backhand.

Forehand Open Receiving

  • Body Movement: Move to the left to create space, potentially using a body feint to the right.
  • Ball Control: Let the ball roll past your body and receive it after it has passed, with feet nearly pointing towards the opponents goal.
  • Stick Position: Keep the flat side of the stick open, ready to control the ball in front of your body.
  • Follow-Up: After receiving, quickly decide whether to pass or accelerate towards the opponents goal, cutting off any defensive passes.

Backhand Open Receiving

  • Body Position: Align your body to receive the ball on your backhand.
  • Stick Rotation: Rotate the stick early, ensuring the flat side is ready to receive.
  • Ball Control: Let the ball roll past your body and receive it just beyond your front foot.
  • Distance: Keep the ball away from your body for more control and room for follow-up actions.
  • Follow-Up: Once the ball is received, you can transition into a dribble, pass, or shot depending on the play situation.

Receiving from the Left

  • Stick Angle: Position the stick at a 45 angle to the ground.
  • Ball Control: Receive the ball near your left foot and use soft hands to slow it down.
  • Body Movement: Turn your left elbow away from your body, guiding the ball towards the right side.
  • Follow-Up: Quickly bring the ball to a dribble position on the forehand, ready for your next move.

Receiving from the Right (Backhand in Front of the Body)

  • Stick Rotation: Rotate the stick so the hook points downwards and the flat side is ready to receive.
  • Stick Position: Hold the stick at a 45 angle to the ground.
  • Elbow Movement: Right elbow and the back of the right hand point in the direction of the ball.
  • Ball Control: Receive the ball in front of or beyond your left foot.
  • Follow-Up: Quickly guide the ball into a normal dribble position on the right side of your body.

Receiving from the Right (Back Foot)

  • Body Position: Turn your upper body to the right to align with the direction of the ball.
  • Stick Position: Hold the stick at a 45 angle, with the flat side open and ready to receive.
  • Ball Control: Receive the ball near your back foot, ensuring you keep it away from the defender.
  • Follow-Up: Quickly guide the ball into a dribble position on the forehand, keeping the ball on your stick and moving forward.

Closed Receiving

  • Purpose: Shield the ball from opponents to maintain possession.
  • Body Position: Face away from the opponents goal, placing your body between the ball and the defender.
  • Stick Position: Keep the ball in front of your body, using both elbows apart to create space and provide protection.
  • Execution: Receive the ball with your back towards the opponent, ensuring the ball stays close to your feet and under control.
  • Follow-Up: Be ready to pivot or pass while maintaining the shielded position.

Receiving and Turning

  • Initial Control: Receive the ball on your forehand while shielding it with your body.
  • Body Movement: As you receive the ball, pivot on your feet and turn quickly to change direction.
  • Stick Control: Maintain close contact with the ball, ensuring it stays on your stick as you turn.
  • Follow-Up: Accelerate out of the turn, either dribbling away from the defender or preparing to pass.

Side-Stop

  • Feint Movement: Approach as if you are going to receive the ball in front of your body by stepping towards it.
  • Execution: At the last moment, step back and let the ball roll in front of your body.
  • Stick Position: Use your stick to stop the ball while turning your left shoulder forward.
  • Follow-Up: Quickly lift or accelerate the ball past the defender after the stop.

Aerial Receiving

  • Stance: Position yourself with feet shoulder-width apart, slightly bent knees for balance.
  • Stick Position: Hold the stick vertically with the flat side facing upwards.
  • Ball Control: Watch the ball closely as it descends, cushioning it with soft hands.
  • Execution: Make contact with the stick slightly above ground level, absorbing the balls momentum.
  • Follow-Up: After receiving, transition smoothly into your next move, whether dribbling, passing, or shooting.

Passing the Defender

Haringtrick (Dutch Move)

  • Approach: Dribble towards the opponent, feint by slightly closing the flat stick side as if moving forward.
  • Execution: Pass the ball to the opponents right and quickly move to their left.
  • Follow-Up: Regain possession and continue with a follow-up move.

Chop (Passing Technique)

  • Setup: Position the ball near or behind your right foot.
  • Action: As the opponent moves towards the ball, hit it quickly, making it jump over their stick.
  • Follow-Up: Accelerate past the opponent.

Receiving Lift

  • Body Position: Get in a balanced stance with knees slightly bent to absorb the balls impact.
  • Stick Angle: Hold the stick with the flat side facing upwards, positioning it at a 45 angle to cushion the lift.
  • Execution: Receive the ball softly by adjusting your hands and stick to absorb the balls momentum in the air.
  • Follow-Up: After receiving the ball, quickly transition into a dribble or prepare for the next play.

Drag/Dummy (Left)

  • Body Position: Approach the ball with knees bent and body low for balance.
  • Stick Control: Drag the ball to the left by using the flat side of the stick.
  • Fake Movement: As you drag, perform a dummy by pretending to continue the movement left, deceiving the defender.
  • Execution: At the last moment, quickly change direction or pass the ball after the dummy, leaving the defender off-balance.

Drag/Dummy (Right)

  • Body Position: Approach the ball low, with knees bent for stability.
  • Stick Control: Use the flat side of the stick to drag the ball to the right.
  • Fake Movement: Pretend to continue the movement to the right, using a dummy to deceive the defender.
  • Execution: Quickly change direction or pass the ball after the dummy to leave the defender wrong-footed.

Triangle Pass (Right)

  • Setup: Begin by positioning yourself and your teammates to form a triangle with two passing options.
  • Execution: Pass the ball to your right, using the flat side of the stick for accuracy.
  • Movement: After passing, move into space to receive the ball again or create another passing lane.
  • Purpose: The triangle pass is used to maintain possession, create space, and move the ball quickly to outmaneuver defenders.

Shuffle

  • Body Position: Stay low with knees slightly bent to maintain balance and agility.
  • Stick Control: Keep the ball close to the stick with soft hands for better control during quick movements.
  • Execution: Rapidly move the ball left and right, using short, sharp touches to maintain possession while evading defenders.
  • Footwork: Use quick, light steps to move your body in sync with the ball, keeping your center of gravity low.
  • Purpose: The shuffle is ideal for maintaining possession and creating space in tight defensive situations.

Dragflick Turn

  • Approach: Start with the ball in front of your body, feet positioned to allow for a smooth pivot.
  • Body Rotation: Rotate your body quickly while keeping the ball on the stick.
  • Stick Movement: Slide the stick under the ball, pushing it forward and slightly upward to build momentum.
  • Execution: As your body turns, flick the ball towards the goal with a snap of the wrists, generating speed and accuracy.
  • Follow-Up: Shift your weight forward and continue moving after the flick to be ready for the next play.

Defending

Tackle Back

  • Approach: Move towards the opponent from behind or the side.
  • Timing: Wait until you are beside the opponent before attempting the tackle to avoid hitting the stick.
  • Execution: Take the ball by hitting it off the opponents stick.
  • Additional Techniques: You can also use a jab, steal, or shave to regain possession.

Frontal Block (Forehand)

  • Approach: Let the opponent approach on your forehand side.
  • Posture: Bend deeply at the knees, keeping your body weight low and forward.
  • Stick Position: Hold the stick horizontally in your left hand, arm extended.
  • Block: Block the ball in the middle of the stick, pushing it forward and left.
  • Follow-Up: Place your right hand on the stick for the next action.

Shave Tackle

  • Approach: Move from behind the opponent.
  • Stick Position: Hold the stick in your left hand, flat side facing up.
  • Execution: Place the stick between the ball and the opponents feet, using a quick wrist movement to shave the ball away.
  • Follow-Up: Pull the ball towards you to continue the play.

Steal

  • Approach: Move towards the opponent from behind.
  • Stick Position: Hold the stick with the flat side half-open, hook pointing downward.
  • Execution: Place the stick over the opponents stick and flick the ball to the right with a quick wrist movement.
  • Follow-Up: Pull the ball towards you for the next move.

Jab on Forehand

  • Technique: One-handed move to regain possession.
  • Stick Position: Hold the stick in your left hand, flat side facing up.
  • Reach: Extend your left arm and front foot for maximum reach.
  • Execution: Stab the ball off the opponents stick with a quick movement.
  • Follow-Up: Step back after the jab to avoid being passed. The jab doesnt guarantee possession but often stops the opponent.

Jab as a Tackle Back Technique

  • Approach: Close in from the side or behind the opponent who has possession of the ball.
  • Stick Position: Hold the stick in the left hand, with the flat side facing upwards.
  • Execution: Extend your left arm and jab quickly to stab the ball away from the opponents stick with minimal contact.
  • Follow-Up: Step back after the jab to avoid being beaten by the opponent, and either regain possession or stop their progress.

Jab Dribble

  • Grip: Hold the stick in your left hand with the arm nearly extended.
  • Wrist Action: Use a quick stabbing motion with your wrist to lift or push the ball forward.
  • Ball Control: Try to keep the ball elevated slightly with each jab, maintaining quick movements.
  • Purpose: The jab dribble helps maintain possession while allowing the player to move past defenders with speed and agility, keeping the ball in play while reducing direct contact with opponents.

Scoring Techniques

Hitting While Turning:

  • Setup: Approach the ball with a balanced stance, preparing for a quick pivot.
  • Body Movement: As you receive or approach the ball, initiate a turn by rotating your body around your planted foot.
  • Stick Position: Maintain control of the stick as you turn, ensuring its ready for a clean hit.
  • Execution: As you complete the turn, time your hit to connect with the ball, using the momentum of the turn to generate power.
  • Follow-Up: Maintain balance and be ready for the next action after the hit.

Hitting While Running:

  • Grip: Maintain a firm grip on the stick, ensuring control while running.
  • Body Balance: Stay low and balanced as you approach the ball, ensuring smooth coordination between your steps and the upcoming hit.
  • Timing: As you run, time your steps so that you hit the ball with precision at the moment of maximum momentum.
  • Execution: Use a controlled yet powerful swing to hit the ball while maintaining forward movement.
  • Follow-Through: Continue running after the hit, ready for the next action.

Hitting from the Right Leg:

  • Stance: Position your body with weight centered on your right leg.
  • Ball Placement: Keep the ball slightly in front of and to the right of your right foot.
  • Body Rotation: Rotate your upper body as you prepare to hit, allowing the weight to shift from your back leg to your front.
  • Swing: Execute a controlled but powerful swing while using the rotation to generate force.
  • Follow-Through: Ensure your follow-through is smooth, allowing the momentum to continue after the hit for balance and accuracy.

Low Long Slap:

  • Body Position: Bend your knees and lower your body to stay close to the ground, ensuring stability.
  • Stick Grip: Hold the stick with both hands close to the top for better control.
  • Ball Position: Place the ball slightly in front and to the side of your body.
  • Swing: Make a wide, low sweeping motion with the stick, keeping it close to the ground to maximize contact area.
  • Follow-Through: Continue the motion after hitting the ball, ensuring accuracy and distance.

High Long Slap:

  • Stance: Lower your body with knees slightly bent, preparing for a sweeping motion.
  • Grip: Hold the stick with hands together near the top, ready to generate power.
  • Ball Position: Place the ball slightly in front and away from your body for a wide swing.
  • Swing: Perform a long, sweeping motion while lifting the stick higher during the follow-through to elevate the ball.
  • Follow-Through: Finish with an upward motion, ensuring the ball lifts while traveling a long distance.

Backhand Long Slap:

  • Stance: Position your body side-on with knees slightly bent.
  • Grip: Hold the stick with both hands near the top, preparing for power generation.
  • Ball Position: Place the ball near or in front of your right foot.
  • Stick Motion: Perform a wide, sweeping motion with the flat side of the stick on the backhand, keeping the stick low to the ground.
  • Follow-Through: Continue the swing after contact, ensuring a smooth and powerful transfer of force.

Slapshot:

  • Stance: Stand with knees bent, feet shoulder-width apart for balance.
  • Grip: Hold the stick firmly with both hands near the top, ready for a powerful strike.
  • Ball Position: Place the ball slightly in front of your body, within reach for a wide swing.
  • Execution: Perform a short backswing and strike the ball with a quick, snapping motion, using the wrists to generate power.
  • Follow-Through: Extend the stick forward after contact, keeping the shot low and fast.

Chip/Chap Slap:

  • Stance: Position yourself with knees bent for balance.
  • Grip: Hold the stick with both hands near the top, ensuring control for a quick snap.
  • Ball Position: Place the ball close to your body.
  • Execution: Perform a quick, sharp slap with a short backswing, focusing on making the ball chip or pop slightly off the ground.
  • Follow-Through: Keep the follow-through short, allowing for quick recovery after the slap.

Drag Flick:

  • Stance: Position your body low with knees bent, feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Ball Placement: Place the ball slightly ahead of your front foot, ready for the flick.
  • Grip: Hold the stick firmly with both hands, hands slightly apart for control and power.
  • Execution: Use a dragging motion, pulling the ball towards you while maintaining contact with the stick. As you approach the flick, rotate your body and wrists to generate lift and speed.
  • Follow-Through: Continue the motion upwards for power and accuracy.

Tip-In:

  • Positioning: Stand near the goal, ready to react quickly to incoming shots or passes.
  • Stick Control: Keep your stick low to the ground, with the flat side ready to deflect or redirect the ball.
  • Execution: As a shot or cross comes in, gently tap or redirect the ball towards the goal with a quick flick of the wrist.
  • Timing: Time your movement precisely to meet the ball, giving it just enough touch to change its direction into the goal.

Reverse Flick

  • Stance: Position your body side-on to the ball with your feet slightly apart.
  • Stick Grip: Hold the stick firmly with both hands, ready to generate power.
  • Ball Position: Place the ball slightly in front of your body and to the left (for right-handed players).
  • Execution: As you approach the ball, rotate your wrists and pull the stick from low to high in a scooping motion.
  • Follow-Up: Snap your wrists at the point of contact, flicking the ball upwards and forward with speed.

Penalty Stroke

  • Stance: Stand behind the ball, feet shoulder-width apart for balance.
  • Ball Placement: Position the ball on the penalty spot, directly in front of the goal.
  • Stick Grip: Hold the stick firmly with both hands, ensuring a solid grip for control.
  • Execution: Take a short backswing, then push or flick the ball towards the goal with power and precision.
  • Follow-Through: Keep your eyes on the ball and extend the follow-through to generate speed, aiming for the corners of the goal for maximum accuracy.

1v1 with Goalkeeper

  • Approach: As you move towards the goalkeeper, maintain control of the ball while assessing the keepers position.
  • Body Position: Stay low with a balanced stance, keeping your stick ready for a quick move.
  • Decision Making: Decide early whether to shoot, dribble around, or fake out the goalkeeper based on their movement.
  • Execution: Use body feints, change of speed, or direction to outmaneuver the goalkeeper.
  • Follow-Up: Once past the goalkeeper, ensure a quick and controlled finish to score.

High Reverse Backhand

  • Stance: Position yourself slightly side-on with feet apart for balance.
  • Grip: Use a firm grip with both hands, ensuring the stick is in reverse position (flat side facing up).
  • Ball Position: Place the ball slightly in front of and to the left of your body.
  • Execution: Swing the stick from low to high, using your wrist to flick the ball upwards.
  • Follow-Through: Finish with an extended follow-through, keeping your eyes on the target to lift the ball high and with power.
